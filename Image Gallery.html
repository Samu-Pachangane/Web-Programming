Indian Cuisines from Different States

Makke di Roti Sarso da Saag - Punjab : This cuisine is made with corn flour and mustard greens that are harvested during winter and served with hot ghee and some jaggery.
Kosha Mangsho - West Beangal : Kosha Mangsho is also called Bengali mutton curry and it is one of the most popular Bengali cuisines due to its delicious taste juicy mutton pieces and different flavors of hot spices.
Misal Pav - Maharashtra : It is a kind of Maharashtrian curry consisting of sprouts, topped with onions and Farsan, and served with Indian dinner rolls (pav).
Papaya Khar - Asaam : It is very popular in Asaam because it contains many health benefits like it boosts immunity, improving eyesight, reducing weight, etc.
Rassam - Tamil Nadu : It is prepared in many South-Indian households and served with hot steamed rice that tastes delicious.
Litti Chokha - Bihar : In this cuisine, Litti is the stuffed whole wheat dough ball and Chokha is a mashed relish made with roasted eggplant, tomato, and boiled potatoes with some green.
Kodi Pulao - Andhra Pradesh : Rice is a staple food of Andhra Pradesh. Kodi Pulao is one of the most special cuisines of Andhra Pradesh due to its spicy blend of rice and meat.
Hyderabadi Biryani - Telangana : It is one of the most popular cuisines in Telangana due to the aromatic flavor of spices and the good color of saffron.
Dhokla - Gujarat : This is a must-try cuisine due to its sweet and salty taste and soft and fluffy texture.
Kafuli - Uttarakhand : It is very beneficial for our overall health due to the presence of spinach which contains a huge amount of nutrition.
Dhaam - Himachal Pradesh : This authentic Himachali thali contains different cuisines from different regions like plain rice, fried pulses or dal, rajma, curd, curry, and different types of desserts that make this thali a wholesome meal.
Fish Curry - Goa : It is most of the most loved cuisine in Goa that tastes super delicious and rich due to the unique flavors of different spices.
Dal Bati Churma - Rajasthan : Dal Bati Churma is one of the most popular typical Rajasthani cuisine containing a three-in-one treat on one plate.
Dhooska - Jharkhand : It is very popular in Jharkhand. It is basically a deep-fried snack served with potato curry, spicy chutney, and fresh salad
Momo - Sikkim : It is basically a bite-size dumpling made with a spoonful of stuffing of either meat or veggies and then cooked in the steaming method.
Chuak - Tripura : It is basically a drink made from fermented rice and water. That’s the reason why it is also called “rice beer”.
Kangshoi - Manipur : In this unique cuisine, they add many seasonal vegetables that are boiled and flavored with sliced onion, cloves, salt, garlic, and ginger.
Appam - Kerala : Appam is usually made up of ground, fermented rice, and coconut batter.
Tunde ke Kabab - Uttar Pradesh : It basically comes from Lucknow which is known as the “city of Nawabs” due to its royal history.
Kadi Pakora - Haryana : Haryanvi people love Kadi Pakora (Curry Pakora) due to its sour and salty taste of kadi and the soft and fluffy texture of pakora dipped in kadi.
Jadoh - Meghalaya : It is a traditional dish of Meghalaya and especially comes from the Khasi tribe from Meghalaya. This cuisine is a kind of biryani prepared in Meghalaya.
Poha - Madhya Pradesh : It is one of the most famous and healthy dishes that come from Madhya Pradesh. This dish is super light and very healthy cuisine made with flattened rice, oil, and some mustard seeds
Chura Sabji - Arunachal Pradesh : It is basically a curry kind of dish made of fermented cheese of either yak milk or cow milk.
Bamboo Fish - Nagaland : This dish is made in bamboo vassal by filling it with fish and some aromatic spices and green chilies, after that it is cooked over fire.
Chilla - Chhattisgarh : This dish is basically made with rice flour batter mixed with the Urad Daal tastes very delicious and is often served with green chutney or tomato sauce.
Bisi Bela Bath - Karnataka : The dish is made by cooking rice, lentils, and vegetables together with a variety of spices, including mustard seeds, cumin, turmeric, and red chili powder.
Misa Mach Poora - Mizoram : It is basically a kind of side dish of Mizoram made with shrimp.
Dalma - Odisha : It is basically a dal recipe made in a different way and tastes super delicious.
Rogan Josh - Kashmir : It is a goat meat recipe cooked in ghee with a very slow cooking method with some authentic Indian spices.

Sweet Dishes of India

Modak : Filled with jaggery, cardamom and poppy seeds, modak is a small, sweet dumpling typically served at Ganesh Chaturthi.
Mango Lassi : mango lassi is simple and wonderfully refreshing on a hot summer day.
Gulab Jamun : Gulab jamun is high on the list of the most popular Indian desserts!
Kulfi : This refreshing cold treat is melt-in-your-mouth delicious. Kulfi is similar to ice cream but thicker, creamier and denser, thanks to its traditional preparation of slow-cooked milk.
Shankarpali : This shankarpali recipe is a perfect example. It combines the crunchy, lightly sweet bite of shakarpara (aka diamond cuts or sweet tukdi) with a seasonal nod to fall.
Rice and Carrot Pudding : gajar ki kheer, this iconic Indian and Pakistani dessert is a true delight.
Sandesh : Mishti is a broad category of desserts traditionally eaten as part of special occasions, religious holidays or weddings Sandesh is one of them.
Besan Ladoo : Ladoo, also known as laddu, are soft dessert balls commonly served for Diwali.
Kheer : Kheer is a indian rice pudding made with rice, milk, and sugar.
Burfi : Burfi (aka barfi) is a word used for several variations of similar sweets. It is often used for flatter, milk-based sweets that are usually cut into squares, diamonds or circles.
Rasgulla : This East Indian delicacy features bite-sized balls of cheese soaked in floral sugar syrup.
Rasmalai : This version is just as delicious with all the traditional rasmalai ingredients like rasgulla (chenna or paneer soaked in sugar), milk, and spices like saffron and cardamom.
Jalebi : Jalebi as the equivalent of American funnel cake: fried, crispy and sugar-coated.
Falooda : Falooda is a popular ice cream dessert made with vermicelli, jelly, rose syrup, sabja seeds, milk and ice cream.
Kalakand : Kalakand is an Indian sweet cheese fudge with a texture similar to an Italian cheesecake.
Nankhatai : Nankhatai are shortbread biscuits popular in northern India. They are eggless cookies that are crisp and light. While traditionally made with all-purpose flour, semolina, sugar and ghee, there are many variants of the snack that play around with flour and spice.
Cham Cham : Cham Cham, also known as chum chum or chom chom, by curdling milk, shaping the results and then coating the treats in a sugar syrup.
Pedha : A popular Indian sweet for Hindu festivals like Diwali, Holi and Raksha Bandhan, pedha are made of milk solids and fragrant, aromatic seasonings like saffron and cardamom.
Soan Papdi : Flaky, light soan papdi first originated in the northern region of India and was traditionally sold loose in paper cones.
Gajar Halwa : If you’re gluten-free or paleo, you’ll have to give this gajar ka halwa recipe a try.
Mysore Pak : The first (and most popular) version is crumbly with a stiffer texture; the other is a bit softer and creamier.
Malpua : The best way to describe malpua is Fried pancakes dunked in a sugary, sweet syrup.
